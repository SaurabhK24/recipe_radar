Tomato and basil croquettes with oozing mozzarella and rocket pesto vinaigrette

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By Tony Tobin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the pesto

  • 10 sun-blushed tomato pieces, roughly chopped
  • 50g/2oz rocket leaves, plus extra to garnish
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts
  • ½ garlic clove
  • 3 piquillo peppers, roughly chopped (available from Spanish delicatessens and some supermarkets)
  • 1 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 5-6 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 tbsp Cabernet Sauvignon vinegar

For the croquettes

  • vegetable oil, for deep fat frying
  • 500g/1lb 2oz potatoes, peeled and cut into large dice
  • 110g/4oz parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • 1 tbsp red pesto (from recipe above)
  • 10 sun-blushed tomato pieces, roughly chopped
  • 10 basil leaves, roughly chopped
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 50g/2oz plain flour plus up to 2 tbsp extra (if too dry)
  • 1 small ball fresh mozzarella cheese, cut into small pieces
  • 110g/4oz breadcrumbs
  • 2 free-range eggs, beaten

Method

  1. For the pesto, place all the pesto ingredients, except the Cabernet Sauvignon vinegar, into a food processor and blend to a fine purée. Set aside.

  2. For the croquettes, preheat the oven to 160C/320F/Gas 2.

  3. Preheat the oil in a deep fat fryer to 160C. (CAUTION: Hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended.)

  4. Place the potatoes into a saucepan of salted water and bring to the boil. Cook for 12-18 minutes, or until tender, then drain and return to the pan.

  5. Place the pan of potatoes back over the heat until all the moisture has evaporated. Mash with a potato masher or ricer until smooth.

  6. Add the parmesan cheese, one tablespoon of the red pesto, and all the sun-blushed tomatoes and basil to the mash and mix well. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste. (Add a little flour if the mixture is too wet to form into shapes with your hands.)

  7. Divide the mixture into 12 equal-sized pieces and shape into cylinder croquettes, using your hands. Push a small piece of mozzarella into the centre of each croquette, closing up the potato to completely cover the mozzarella.

  8. Place the remaining flour and the breadcrumbs onto two separate plates. Place the eggs in a bowl. Dredge the croquettes in the flour, then dip them into the beaten eggs and finally roll them in the breadcrumbs, to cover, retaining the shape.

  9. Carefully lower a few croquettes at a time into the preheated deep fat fryer and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until golden-brown and crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper. Transfer the croquettes to a roasting tray and place in the preheated oven to keep warm.

  10. To serve, add the Cabernet Sauvignon vinegar to the remaining pesto and place a spoonful onto each of four plates. Place three croquettes on top of each dollop of pesto and garnish with rocket.
