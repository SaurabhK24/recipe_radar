Braised pork ribs with red wine sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

1 person

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 1kg/2¼lb pork ribs, cut into 5cm/2in-thick ribs
  • 50ml/2fl oz olive oil
  • 1 onion, peeled, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, peeled, thinly sliced
  • 1 stick celery, finely chopped
  • 300ml/10fl oz red wine
  • 250ml/9fl oz chicken stock
  • 250ml/9fl oz passata
  • 2 salted anchovy fillets
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • crusty bread, to serve

Method

  1. Parboil the ribs in a pan of water for 20-25 minutes, or until tender. Drain well and pat dry with kitchen paper. Set aside to cool.

  2. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Add the onion, carrot and celery and fry for 6-8 minutes, or until softened.

  3. Add the wine, chicken stock, passata, anchovies and rosemary sprigs, stir well and bring the mixture to the boil.

  4. Add the ribs, then reduce the heat until the mixture is simmering. Simmer for 45-90 minutes, checking regularly, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone. (NB: Add a little water to the pan during cooking if it starts to dry out.)

  5. To serve, divide the ribs equally among six serving plates. Spoon over the red wine sauce. Serve with crusty bread.
