Turkey saltimbocca

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

1 person

Turkey breast is given the Italian treatment. Prosciutto adds flavour and a white wine sauce keeps everything nicely succulent.

By Brian Turner
From Christmas Kitchen with James Martin

Ingredients

  • 2 turkey breast medallions
  • 12 small slices prosciutto
  • 12 small sage leaves
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 115g/4oz unsalted butter
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 tbsp chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp dry white wine
  • squeeze fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley

Method

  1. Place each turkey medallion between sheets of cling film and use a rolling pin to flatten them.

  2. Discard the cling film and lay a sage leaf and a slice of prosciutto on each piece of turkey. Secure in place with cocktail sticks.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a pan and then add 25g/1oz of the butter. Cook the medallions, prosciutto side up, then turn over and cook the other side. Remove from the pan and place on kitchen paper. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Remove any excess fat from the pan and add the chicken stock and white wine. Bring to the boil and then simmer until the volume of liquid is reduced by half. Strain into a clean pan and add a squeeze of lemon juice.

  5. Add the rest of the butter, taste and check the seasoning is to your liking.

  6. Add the parsley and serve with the turkey.
