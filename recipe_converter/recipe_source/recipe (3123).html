Derry duck and Londonderry potatoes

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

2 people

Duck breasts in an orange glaze with sautéed potatoes and caramelised apples - plus a side of kale to cut through the richness.

Equipment and preparation: for this recipe you will need a Parisian scoop (also known as a melon baller).

By Paul Rankin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the squash purée

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 large squash, peeled and cut into 2cm/¾in dice
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated root ginger
  • 1 orange, zest only
  • 100ml/3½fl oz double cream

For the caramelised apples

  • 2 braeburn apples, peeled
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice, for acidulated water
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar

For the duck glaze

  • 100ml/3½fl oz maple syrup
  • 100ml/3½fl oz soy sauce
  • 100ml/3½fl oz balsamic vinegar
  • 100ml/3½fl oz orange juice

For the Derry duck

  • 400g/14oz new potatoes
  • 4 duck breasts, trimmed, butterflied and skin scored
  • 200g/7oz curly kale, roughly chopped
  • 50g/1¾oz unsalted butter
  • 50g/1¾oz duck fat (reserved from cooking the duck breast)
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. For the squash purée, heat the oil and butter in a large frying pan. Once the butter has melted add the squash and gently fry without colouring. Add the ginger and orange zest and cook until the squash is soft. Once softened, add the cream and season with salt and pepper.

  2. Transfer the cooked squash to a blender and purée until smooth. Pass through a fine sieve into a clean pan to remove any lumps.

  3. For caramelised apples, take a Parisian scoop and cut out spheres of apple and place them in a bowl of acidulated water.

  4. Place a frying pan on the heat and add the butter and sugar. Once melted and the sugar had started to caramelise, drain the apple balls well and add them to the pan. Coat in the caramel, then remove them from the heat and set aside to cool.

  5. For the duck glaze, boil the maple syrup, soy sauce and balsamic vinegar in a pan until reduced to a sticky glaze. Add the orange juice and reduce again.

  6. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 4.

  7. For the Derry duck, put the potatoes in a large saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring to the boil and cook the potatoes until soft but not falling apart. Once cooked, remove from the pan and refresh in ice-cold water to stop the cooking process. Carefully peel the cooled potatoes and set aside.

  8. Season the duck breasts with salt and pepper, then fry them skin-side down in an ovenproof frying pan (you may need to do this in batches). Gently cook for about 8 minutes until caramelised all over and the fat has rendered from the skin. Pour the fat from the pan into small bowl. Transfer the duck to the oven and cook for 3 minutes.

  9. Remove the pan from the oven and add the some of the glaze, coat the duck in the glaze, then set aside to rest for 10 minutes before serving.

  10. Heat a large frying pan and add the butter, once melted add the chopped kale and toss to coat. Cook until the kale is tender, then season with salt and pepper.

  11. Heat the duck fat in a separate large pan and add the peeled potatoes. Cook gently until golden-brown on all sides. Season with salt and pepper.

  12. To serve, reheat the caramelised apples and squash purée. Spoon the purée onto serving plates. Slice the duck breasts and place next to the purée. Add the apples next to the duck and the wilted greens in the middle.
