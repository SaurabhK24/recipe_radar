Ham shank croquettes

Preparation time

over 2 hours

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 250g/9oz ham, picked from a ready-cooked ham shank
  • 250g/9oz potatoes, boiled until tender, drained, then mashed
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 chilli, finely chopped
  • 4 gherkins, finely chopped
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 150g/5oz plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 free-range eggs, beaten
  • 150g/5oz polenta
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

For the sauce

  • 25g/1oz butter, unsalted
  • 25g/1oz plain flour
  • 200ml/7fl oz whole milk
  • 3 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • ½ lemon, juice only
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Place the ham, mashed potato, parsley, chilli and gherkins into a bowl and mix to combine well. Season well with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  2. With floured hands, mould small amounts of the mixture into 7.5cm/3in long cylinders.

  3. Dredge the croquettes in the flour, then dip into the beaten egg and finally dip into the polenta to coat completely.

  4. Heat the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Add the croquettes and fry, turning occasionally, until crisp and golden-brown all over. Remove from the pan and drain on kitchen paper.

  5. For the sauce, melt the butter in a pan over a medium heat, then add the flour. Cook, stirring well, for 2-3 minutes.

  6. Add the milk, a little at a time, stirring constantly. Keep adding the milk and cooking until you have a smooth sauce.

  7. Add the parsley and lemon juice, then season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  8. To serve, place a few croquettes onto each plate and drizzle with the sauce.
