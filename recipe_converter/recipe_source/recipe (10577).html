Tuna burger with chips and roasted tomatoes

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

By Lesley Waters
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the tuna burger

  • 100g/3½oz tuna steak
  • 2 salad onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • pinch caster sugar
  • 2 tsp sesame seeds
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the chips

  • 100g/3½oz new potatoes, blanched and halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the roasted tomatoes

  • 4 tomatoes halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. To make the burger, place all of the burger ingredients into a food processor and blend to a thick paste. Remove the mixture from the processor and shape into a burger.

  3. Heat a griddle pan until smoking, place the patty onto the griddle and cook for three minutes on each side, or until cooked through.

  4. To make the chips, place the potatoes and the oil into a large bowl, mix together and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  5. Place the potatoes onto a small baking sheet and place into the oven to roast for 15-20 minutes, until crisp and golden.

  6. For the roasted tomatoes, place the tomatoes onto a small baking sheet. Drizzle over the oil and balsamic vinegar and sprinkle over the dried chilli flakes. Season well with salt and freshly ground black pepper and place into the oven to roast for 8-10 minutes, until softened.

  7. To serve, place the tuna burger on a warm plate with some of the tomatoes on top and the chips alongside.
