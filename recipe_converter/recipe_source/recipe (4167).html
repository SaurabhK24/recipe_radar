Grilled mackerel with potato and onion mustard

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

2 people

By Mike Robinson
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 2 fresh mackerel
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice only
  • olive oil

For the potato salad

  • 600g/1lb 5oz new potatoes
  • 1 tsp wholegrain mustard
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 big bulb of fennel, finely sliced
  • 1 red onion, finely sliced
  • small bunch of flatleaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Preheat a grill to a medium-high heat.

  2. Fillet the mackerel and remove all the rib bones. You can ask your fishmonger to do this for you.

  3. Season the mackerel well, squeeze a few drops of lemon juice and a sprinkling of zest over each fillet, and drizzle them with olive oil.

  4. Place under the grill, skin side up, for five minutes. After five minutes turn them over to brown the meat which will be nearly cooked by now and should only need a minute or two. Set aside to rest.

  5. To make the salad, boil the potatoes until firm but cooked, and cut them in half. Put them in a mixing bowl with a splash of olive oil, the rest of the lemon juice and zest, and the mustard and vinegar.

  6. Mix together well, then add the finely sliced onion and fennel. Add a good amount of the parsley and season well.

  7. Divide the salad between four serving plates to form a mound. Lay the mackerel fillets on top, and pour over the juices from the fish and the bottom of the salad bowl.
