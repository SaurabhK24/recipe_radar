Chocolate and caramel puddings

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

1 person

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 120g/4oz unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 120g/4oz dark chocolate (minimum 70 per cent cocoa solids)
  • 4 free-range eggs
  • 220g/7½oz caster sugar
  • 75g/2½oz plain flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 50g/2oz cocoa powder

For the caramel sauce

  • 200g/7oz sugar
  • 100g/3½oz butter
  • 100ml/3½fl oz double cream
  • whipped or clotted cream, to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/360F/Gas 4.

  2. Grease four ovenproof coffee or tea cups (or ramekins) with butter.

  3. Place the dark chocolate and the butter into a heatproof bowl placed over a pan of barely simmering water (make sure the water doesn't touch the bottom of the bowl).

  4. Stir the chocolate and butter mixture until melted and combined, then remove from the heat and allow to cool slightly.

  5. Beat the eggs in a separate bowl until foamy, then add the sugar and beat until pale and thick.

  6. Gradually add the melted chocolate and butter mixture to the eggs, stirring continuously.

  7. Sift the flour, baking powder and cocoa powder together, then fold into the chocolate and egg mixture.

  8. Pour the mixture into the prepared cups and bake for 8-12 minutes (depending on the size of the cups), or until cooked but still gooey in the middle.

  9. For the sauce, melt the sugar in a heavy-based pan over a medium heat, stirring continuously.

  10. Add the butter and whisk well until combined, then remove from the heat and whisk in the cream.

  11. To serve, place the puddings in their cups onto small serving plates. Pour over the caramel sauce and finish with a dollop of whipped or clotted cream.
