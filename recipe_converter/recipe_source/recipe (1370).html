Caramelised apricot and sultana tarte tatin

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the tarte tatin

  • 2 tbsp caster sugar
  • 30g/1oz butter
  • 4 apricots, halved, stones removed
  • 2 tbsp sultanas
  • flour, for dusting
  • 100g/3½oz ready-made puff pastry

For the almond brittle

  • 85g/3oz caster sugar
  • 50g/2oz whole almonds
  • 4 scoops vanilla ice cream, to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. For the tarte tatin, heat the sugar in a small ovenproof frying pan over a low heat until melted.

  3. Add the butter and heat until the mixture is caramel coloured.

  4. Add the apricot halves cut-side up, scatter with the sultanas and cook gently for 2-3 minutes, or until softened.

  5. Roll the pastry out on a lightly floured surface to the thickness of a one pound coin.

  6. Cut out a circle of pastry the same size as the circumference of the frying pan. Place the pastry on top of the apricots and press down around the edges to seal in the filling.

  7. Transfer to the oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the pastry is golden-brown on top. Remove from the oven and leave to cool slightly.

  8. Slide a palette knife around the edge of the tart, then carefully turn out onto a serving plate.

  9. For the almond brittle, place the sugar into a non-stick frying pan and heat gently. When the sugar has melted and is beginning to turn a caramel colour, add the almonds and stir well to coat them all.

  10. Pour onto a non-stick mat or baking sheet and leave to cool. When cool, break into pieces.

  11. To serve, cut the tarte tatin into generous wedges and place onto serving plates with a scoop of ice cream and a sprinkling of almond brittle.
