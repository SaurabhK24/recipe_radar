Fish cakes with chive beurre blanc

You need JavaScript to view this clip.

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 6

These luxury fish cakes with a rich homemade sauce are decadent enough for a special occasion.

By James Martin
From Home Comforts at Christmas

Ingredients

For the fish cakes

  • 400g/14oz floury potatoes, diced
  • 1 lemon, thickly sliced, plus a squeeze of lemon juice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 onion, thickly sliced
  • 300g/10½oz boneless, skinless salmon fillet
  • 300g/10½oz boneless, skinless smoked haddock
  • 200g/7oz raw tiger prawns, peeled and de-veined
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh dill
  • 4 spring onions, trimmed, finely sliced
  • 50g/1¾oz butter
  • 300g/10½oz rainbow chard, larger stalks removed

For the beurre blanc

  • 1 large shallot, peeled, finely sliced
  • 200ml/7fl oz white wine
  • 250g/9oz chilled butter, cut into cubes
  • squeeze lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh chives
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Boil the potatoes in a saucepan of salted water for 12-15 minutes, or until very tender, then drain and return to the pan. Return to the heat for a couple of minutes to drive off any excess moisture. Set aside until cool.

  2. Pour enough water into a saucepan to reach 5cm/2in up the sides. Add the lemon, bay leaf and onion and bring to the boil. Add the fish and prawns and simmer for 3-4 minutes, or until just cooked through, then remove from the heat and leave the fish to cool in the cooking liquid.

  3. Meanwhile, for the beurre blanc, heat the shallot and white wine in a saucepan until bubbling. Reduce the heat and simmer until there is only about 50ml/2fl oz liquid left in the pan.

  4. Whisk in the butter, a little at a time, waiting until most of the butter has melted and incorporated into the mixture before adding more.

  5. Once all of the butter has been added, add a squeeze of lemon juice and strain the mixture into a clean saucepan. Stir in the chives and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Cover with cling film and set aside at room temperature until needed.

  6. When the fish and potatoes are cool, flake the fish into a large bowl, discarding any skin and bones. Roughly chop the prawns and add them to the bowl. Add the chopped dill and spring onions and season with a squeeze of lemon juice.

  7. Add the potatoes and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Carefully combine the mixture with your hands, taking care not to break up the fish too much.

  8. Form the mixture into 6 equal-sized patties. Chill in the fridge on a plate for 5 minutes.

  9. Meanwhile, heat half of the butter in a frying pan over a medium heat until foaming. Add the chard and sauté for 3-4 minutes until wilted, then season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Drain onto kitchen paper then clean the pan with a piece of kitchen paper and return it to the heat.

  10. Add the remaining butter to the pan. When foaming, add the fish cakes, in batches if necessary, and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden-brown on both sides and piping hot throughout.

  11. Just before serving, remove the cling film from the beurre blanc and heat through.

  12. To serve, spoon the chard onto serving plates, top each portion with a fish cake and spoon the chive beurre blanc around the edge.

Recipe Tips

If you are making the fish cakes in advance or in a large batch, fry until just coloured on each side then set aside to cool. When cool, chill in the fridge or freeze (defrost before cooking). When ready to use, preheat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6 and place the fish cakes onto a roasting tray. Bake in the oven for 15 minutes until piping hot throughout.
