Scones with strawberry compôte and clotted cream

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 8-12 scones

Recommended by

10 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

A quick strawberry compôte turns these scones into a rather delicious afternoon tea.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the scones

  • 225g/8oz self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • pinch salt
  • 25g/1oz caster sugar
  • 50g/1¾oz unsalted butter, softened
  • 150ml/5fl oz whole milk
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten for glazing

For the strawberry compôte

  • 250g/9oz strawberries, hulled and cut in half
  • 100g/3½oz caster sugar

To serve

  • 100g/3½oz butter, softened
  • 250g/9oz clotted cream

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/Gas 7. Lightly grease a baking tray.

  2. Sift together the flour, baking powder and salt into a bowl. Stir in the sugar and add the butter. Using your fingertips, quickly rub the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs.

  3. Add the milk, a little at a time, working to form a smooth dough. If possible, rest the dough for 5-15 minutes before rolling.

  4. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured work surface until 2cm/¾in thick. Using a 5cm/2in pastry cutter, cut the dough, using one sharp tap and not twisting the dough as you cut. Twisting the scone mix will result in an uneven rising.

  5. Once cut, the scones can be either brushed with the beaten egg for a shiny glaze, or dusted with flour for a matt finish.

  6. Place the scones on the baking tray and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden-brown.

  7. For the strawberry compôte, place the strawberries, sugar and 60ml/2fl oz water in a small saucepan and simmer for 5 minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside to cool and thicken.

  8. To serve, leave the scones to cool, then split in half and add butter, jam and clotted cream to serve.

Recipe Tips

If you have a food processor or freestanding mixer, using butter and flour which has been chilled in the fridge will result in 'shorter' scones.
