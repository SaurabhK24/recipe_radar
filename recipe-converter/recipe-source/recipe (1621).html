Cheddar, smoked bacon and courgette quiche

You need JavaScript to view this clip.

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 6

These homemade mini quiches are perfect for parties, picnics, or as a starter to a meal with friends.

For this recipe you will need 6 x 9cm/3½in loose-bottomed tart tins.

By James Martin
From James Martin: Home Comforts

Ingredients

For the pastry

  • 250g/9oz plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 150g/5½oz cold butter, cubed, plus extra for greasing
  • pinch salt
  • 1 free-range egg

For the filling

  • 6 slices dry-cured smoked streaky bacon
  • 5 free-range egg yolks
  • 300ml/10fl oz double cream
  • 150g/5½oz mature cheddar, finely grated
  • 1 small courgette, trimmed, finely diced
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only

For the salad

  • 1 tsp wholegrain mustard
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • 3 tsp rapeseed oil
  • small bag green salad leaves
  • 1 head Little Gem lettuce, leaves washed and separated

Method

  1. For the pastry, in a large bowl, rub together the flour and butter with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Season with a pinch of salt.

  2. Add the beaten egg and bring the mixture together into a ball, then knead very lightly on a lightly floured work surface.

  3. Wrap in greaseproof paper and cling film, then chill in the fridge for 10-15 minutes to firm up.

  4. Meanwhile, for the filling, preheat the oven to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 4. Grease 6 small, loose-bottomed tart tins, each with a diameter of 9cm/3½in diameter and a height of 4cm/1½in.

  5. Roll the pastry out onto a lightly floured surface to a thickness of 3mm. Cut 6 discs, each slightly larger in diameter than the tart cases, from the pastry (you will need to re-roll the pastry off-cuts for the last two discs).

  6. Line each tin with a pastry disc, pressing gently into the base and sides, then trim the sides so that the pastry cases are flush to the edge of the tins.

  7. Dry fry the bacon in a frying pan over a medium heat for 3-4 minutes, or until golden-brown and just crisp. Drain on kitchen paper, then roughly chop and set aside.

  8. Whisk together the eggs and cream in a jug, then season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  9. Sprinkle half the cheese equally among the tart cases, followed by half of the chopped bacon.

  10. Divide all of the diced courgette among the pastry cases, then cover with the remaining cheese and bacon. Garnish with the thyme leaves.

  11. Pour the egg and cream mixture into the tart cases, then bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the filling has just set and is golden-brown on top. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool slightly before serving.

  12. Meanwhile, for the salad, shake the mustard, vinegar and rapeseed oil in a small jar until well combined.

  13. Fill a resealable plastic bag with the salad leaves, add the dressing and shake the bag to coat the leaves in the dressing. Alternatively, pour the dressing over the salad leaves in a bowl and mix well.

  14. Serve the quiches warm with the salad alongside.

Recipe Tips

Tip: To make it easier to transfer the quiches to the oven without spilling the filling, fill the tart cases with the dry ingredients, then place them onto a baking tray and set the baking tray onto the edge of the oven shelf. Fill the tart cases with the cream mixture, then just slide the baking tray into the oven.
