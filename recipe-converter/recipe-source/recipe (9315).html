Spicy chickpea burgers with mango salsa

You need JavaScript to view this clip.

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

8 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

A simple veggie burger with a nice kick of spice. Serve with a zesty mango salsa for a snack that's full of flavour.

Each serving provides 450kcal, 16g protein, 67g carbohydrate (of which 9g sugars), 11g fat (of which 1.5g saturates), 7.5g fibre and 1.3g salt.

By Shelina Permalloo

Ingredients

  • 400g tin chickpeas in water
  • 2 free-range eggs
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 100g/3½oz breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • salt and pepper

For the mango salsa

  • 1 mango
  • 1 spring onion
  • 1 lime, juice only
  • ½ tsp dried mint
  • pinch chilli flakes

To serve

  • 4 wholemeal pittas

Method

  1. Drain the chickpeas and tip them into a bowl. Crack in the eggs. Add the curry powder and breadcrumbs, season with salt and pepper, then mash together using a potato masher or fork until you have a rough paste.

  2. Using your hands, shape the mixture into 8 small burger patties. Heat the oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat and cook the patties for 2-3 minutes on each side. Remove from the pan and drain on a plate lined with kitchen paper.

  3. In the meantime, stone, peel and dice the mango and put it into a small mixing bowl. Thinly slice the spring onion and add it to the bowl. Add the dried mint and chilli flakes and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Preheat the grill to high. Slice the pittas in half and lightly toast them. Serve the burgers with the pittas and salsa.
