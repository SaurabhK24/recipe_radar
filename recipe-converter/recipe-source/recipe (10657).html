Udon noodles in miso broth with stir-fried greens and shichimi pepper

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 1

Recommended by

1 person

This healthy noodle soup combines heady “umami” Japanese flavours. You can find most of the ingredients in an Asian supermarket.

By The Hairy Bikers
From The Hairy Bikers' Cook Off

Ingredients

For the miso broth

  • 5cm/2in piece kombu seaweed
  • 1½ tsp dashi powder
  • 2½cm/1in piece fresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1 tbsp white miso paste
  • pinch caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 75g/2½oz tofu, cubed into 1cm/½in pieces

For the stir-fried greens

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 garlic clove, finely sliced
  • 4 shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 heads bok choi, sliced in quarters
  • 75g/2½oz kale, shredded
  • 1 small courgette, sliced
  • 50g/2¾oz sugar snap peas
  • 2 tbsp Japanese soy sauce
  • 40g/2½oz enoki mushrooms, broken into pieces
  • 4 spring onions, finely sliced
  • 1 tsp shichimi pepper
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1 x 150g/5oz packet udon noodles

Method

  1. For the miso broth, place a saucepan of water over a medium heat and add the kombu seaweed.

  2. Add the dashi powder, stir and leave to simmer for a few minutes. Add the grated ginger, white miso paste, sugar and mirin. Taste and adjust the amount of sugar or mirin if necessary. Keep warm.

  3. For the stir-fried greens, heat a wok with a little vegetable oil until shimmering, add the sliced garlic and fry gently for 10 seconds. Tip in the sliced mushrooms, bok choi, kale, courgette and sugar snap peas.

  4. Keep stirring the contents of the wok and cook for two minutes or so, then add the soy sauce, enoki mushrooms and all but a teaspoon of the spring onions.

  5. Meanwhile, bring a second pan, filled with water, to the boil.

  6. Fry the vegetables until slightly softened, then add all but a pinch of the schicimi pepper and sesame seeds.

  7. Drop the udon noodles into the boiling water and cook for a few minutes, or until heated through, and then drain.

  8. To finish the miso broth, add the tofu cubes to the broth.

  9. To serve, lay the udon noodles in the bottom of a bowl, place the stir-fried greens on top and then ladle over the miso broth. Finish with the reserved finely sliced spring onion and a pinch of both the shichimi and sesame seeds.

Recipe Tips

Shichimi pepper is a Japanese spice blend made up of seven different spices.
