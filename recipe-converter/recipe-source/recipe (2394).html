Cinnamon baked pear with rich chocolate sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the pear

  • 55g/2oz butter
  • 115g/4oz plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 55g/2oz caster sugar
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten
  • 1 pear, peeled
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp demerara sugar

For the chocolate sauce

  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 100ml/3½fl oz boiling water
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar

To serve

  • 100ml/3½fl oz double cream, lightly whipped
  • 1 sprig fresh mint

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. For the pear, place the butter and flour into a food processor and pulse until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Add the caster sugar and most of the beaten egg (reserve some to use as an eggwash), pulsing until the mixture comes together to form a soft dough.

  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured work surface and roll out into a thin circle. Place the peeled pear upright in the centre. Draw up the edges and secure at the top, pressing the dough together at the base of the pear stalk and sealing with some of the remaining beaten egg. Brush with the rest of the egg and sprinkle over the cinnamon and sugar. Place onto a baking tray and bake in the oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the pastry is golden-brown.

  4. For the sauce, place the cocoa, water and sugar in a small pan and simmer for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently, or until the sugar has dissolved and the sauce is smooth and creamy.

  5. To serve, spoon some of the whipped cream onto the centre of a serving plate and drizzle around the chocolate sauce, feathering it with a fork. Place the pear in the centre, and garnish with fresh mint to serve.
