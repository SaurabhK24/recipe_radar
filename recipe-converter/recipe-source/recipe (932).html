Blueberry butter

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Makes 3 x 250ml/9fl oz jars

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

Apple butter is a classic American preserve of slow cooked puréed apples – it’s a great way to use up a glut of apples. This version adds blueberries for a change.

By Jo Wesley and Avril Rogers
From The Big Allotment Challenge

Ingredients

  • 500g/1lb 2oz cooking apples (about 3)
  • 250g/9oz blueberries
  • 300ml/10fl oz clear apple juice
  • 1 medium orange, zest only
  • 350g/12oz sugar

Method

  1. Wash the apples, then chop them into small chunks – there is no need to peel or core them – and put into a preserving pan.

  2. Wash the blueberries and place into the preserving pan with the apples and apple juice.

  3. Add the orange zest.

  4. Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes or until the apples have collapsed, stirring occasionally.

  5. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve, then return the blueberry and apple mixture to the pan.

  6. Add the sugar and return to the heat. Stir constantly until the sugar is dissolved.

  7. Continue to cook until a thick, creamy consistency is achieved – this can take up to an hour. Stir from time to time.

  8. Pour into sterilised jars. Cover with a lid and label.

Recipe Tips

Store the butter in the fridge and eat within a week.
