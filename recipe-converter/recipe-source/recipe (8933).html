Slow-roast shoulder of pork with quinces served with savoy cabbage and roasted potato wedges

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 6

By Sophie Grigson
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 1.25-1.5 kg/3lb boned shoulder of pork
  • 550g/1lb 3oz quinces (2 fairly large ones)
  • 2 red onions, halved and thickly sliced
  • 2 medium chillies, seeded and cut into strips
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 30g/1¼oz sugar
  • 30g/1¼oz light muscovado sugar
  • 100ml/3½fl oz white wine
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

To serve

  • savoy cabbage
  • roasted potato wedges

Method

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. If the shoulder has been 'rolled', remove the string and flatten out. Using a sharp knife make criss-cross slashes about 2-3cm/0.5-1in apart in the skin.

  3. Lightly grease a baking dish. Lay the pork in it skin-side up.

  4. Peel the quinces then quarter and core, cutting each quarter into two or three slices depending on size.

  5. Scatter the quince slices, onion, chillies, garlic and bay leaves into the baking dish around the pork.

  6. Mix the oil, sugars, wine, vinegar, salt and pepper and spoon over the pork and quinces and onions.

  7. Cover with foil and place in the oven.

  8. Roast for one hour, remove the foil and baste both pork and quinces and onions with their own juices.

  9. Return to the oven, uncovered, for a further hour, basting occasionally.

  10. When the pork is very tender, and the quinces are as soft as butter turn off the oven. Prop the door ajar and leave to rest for 10-20 minutes before carving and serving.

  11. Serve with savoy cabbage and roasted potato wedges.
