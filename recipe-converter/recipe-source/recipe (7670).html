Rhubarb and star anise crumble

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 6-8

Recommended by

6 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 450g/1lb rhubarb
  • 200ml/7fl oz orange juice (preferably freshly squeezed)
  • 125g/4oz caster sugar
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick, broken in half
  • 1 vanilla pod, split

For the topping

  • 75g/2¾oz plain flour
  • 50g/2oz demerara sugar
  • 50g/2oz flaked almonds
  • pinch ground cinnamon
  • 50g/2oz butter, unsalted

To serve

  • hot custard

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/355F/Gas 4.

  2. Cut the rhubarb into 5cm/2in pieces and lay in a 22cm/9in square ovenproof dish.

  3. Meanwhile place the orange juice, sugar, star anise, cinnamon and vanilla pod into a saucepan over a medium heat and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until the liquid thickens to a syrup, then pour the syrup over the rhubarb.

  4. For the topping, place the flour, sugar, almonds and cinnamon into a bowl and mix well. Add the butter and rub it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

  5. Sprinkle the topping over the rhubarb and transfer to the oven to bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden-brown.

  6. To serve, spoon out generous portions of the rhubarb crumble into bowls and serve with custard.
