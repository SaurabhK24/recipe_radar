Scones with marrons glacés and chocolate sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the scones with marrons glacés

  • 225g/8oz self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 55g/2oz butter
  • 30g/1oz caster sugar
  • 150ml/¼ pint milk, plus extra for brushing
  • 5 roasted chestnuts in syrup

For the chocolate sauce

  • 55g/2oz dark chocolate, melted
  • 100ml/3½fl oz double cream
  • icing sugar, to dust

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 220C/425F/Gas 7.

  2. For the scones, place the flour, baking powder and salt into a food processor with the butter and caster sugar. Blend until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Add the milk and blend until the mixture forms a dough.

  3. Turn out the dough onto a floured surface and knead until the mixture is smooth. Roll or pat into a block 2.5cm/1in thick. Using a floured round cutter, cut into five scones.

  4. Press a chestnut into the top of each scone and brush with a little milk.

  5. Place the scones onto a non-stick baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes, or until well-risen and golden-brown.

  6. For the sauce, melt the chocolate with the double cream in a mixing bowl set over a pan of just-simmering water (make sure that the bowl does not touch the surface of the water). Stir until melted and well-combined.

  7. To serve, dust the scones with icing sugar and drizzle over the hot chocolate sauce.
