Gingerbread biscuits

Preparation time

over 2 hours

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 15-20

Recommended by

7 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

A great thing to make at Christmas, these simple biscuits look impressive hanging from the Christmas tree.

By Donal Skehan

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp golden syrup
  • 60g/2¼oz caster sugar
  • 50g/1¾oz butter
  • 3 tbsp double cream
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 175g/6oz plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda

Method

  1. Heat the golden syrup, sugar and butter into a medium saucepan over a medium heat. Stir until the mixture is heated and the sugar completely dissolved. Remove from the heat and add the cream and spices.

  2. Add the flour and bicarbonate of soda, stirring as you add it until you have a loose mixture. Place a sheet of parchment paper on a baking tray and tip the mixture on top. Cover with another piece of parchment and flatten the dough slightly. Leave to cool in the fridge for 2-3 hours. After this you should have a thick dough.

  3. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  4. Remove the dough from the fridge and roll between the sheets of paper with a rolling pin until 1cm/½in thick.

  5. Dust a work surface with flour and roll the dough out with a floured rolling pin until 5mm/¼in thick (as you want the biscuits to only rise slightly when cooking and be nice and crisp when cooked).

  6. Using a floured cutter of your choice cut out the biscuits and place onto a lined baking tray. You may need to cook these in batches or use two trays if they don’t all fit on one tray. Any scraps of dough can be pressed together, rerolled and cut out .

  7. Place the biscuits in the preheated oven and bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the biscuits are a rich brown colour at the sides and slightly paler in the centre.

  8. Remove from the oven. If you intend to hang the biscuits up (say from a Christmas tree) pierce a small hole in the top of the biscuit immediately after they come out of the oven and place on a wire rack to cool.

  9. Once cool, you can thread ribbon or string through the hole at the top of the biscuit to hang from the tree. Store in an airtight container so that the biscuits remain crisp.
