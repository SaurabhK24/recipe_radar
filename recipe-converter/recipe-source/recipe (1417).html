Coffee and cardamom cake with pistachio cream

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 8-10

Recommended by

21 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

Try James Martin's coffee cake with a difference: flavoured with aromatic cardamom and served with a delicious pistachio cream.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the cake

  • 200g/7oz butter, softened, plus extra for greasing
  • 200g/7oz caster sugar
  • 4 free-range eggs
  • 3 tbsp strong coffee
  • 8 cardamom pods, seeds removed and ground
  • 250g/9oz self-raising flour

For the filling

  • 250g/9fl oz double cream
  • 1 tbsp icing sugar
  • 5 cardamom pods, seeds removed and ground
  • 60g/2½oz pistachios, shells removed, roughly chopped

For the icing

  • 110g/4oz icing sugar
  • 1 tbsp warm coffee

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 190C/375F/Gas 5.

  2. Grease and line a 23cm/9in deep cake tin.

  3. Place the butter and sugar into a bowl and whisk until light and fluffy.

  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating between each addition until well incorporated.

  5. Add the coffee and ground cardamom seeds and mix well, then fold in the flour.

  6. Pour the mixture into the cake tin, transfer to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden-brown and well risen. Remove from the oven and allow to cool.

  7. For the filling, place the cream into a bowl and whisk until soft peaks form when the whisk is removed.

  8. Add the icing sugar, ground cardamom seeds and pistachios and whisk gently until the mixture forms firm peaks.

  9. For the icing, place the icing sugar into a bowl, add the coffee and mix together until smooth.

  10. To assemble the cake, carefully cut the cake in half horizontally with a bread knife and place the bottom half onto a plate. Spread the pistachio cream over the bottom half of the cake, then top with the other half. Spoon over the icing, allowing it to drizzle down the sides of the cake.

  11. To serve, cut the cake into wedges and place onto serving plates.
