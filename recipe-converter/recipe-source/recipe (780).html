Beetroot layer cake

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

10 people

This lovely savoury bake makes a great winter supper. Serve with a simple salad dressed with good oil.

By Antonio Carluccio
From Great British Food Revival

Ingredients

  • 400g/1lb beetroot
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp English mustard
  • 100g/3½oz leek, finely chopped
  • 50g/1¾oz butter
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 425ml/15fl oz milk
  • nutmeg, grated
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 100g/3½oz mature Cheddar
  • 200g/7oz smoked roasted ham
  • parmesan, grated

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4. Boil the beetroot in slightly salted water, peel and cut into 3mm/⅛in slices. Place the beetroot into a bowl and mix with two tablespoons of the olive oil and mustard. Fry the leek in the remaining oil until soft.

  2. Melt the butter in a saucepan over a medium heat, stir in the flour then add the milk in a steady stream until it is all incorporated. Add a grating of nutmeg and a pinch of black pepper then the Cheddar and finally the leeks. Mix until well combined and remove the pan from the heat.

  3. Put a layer of beetroot in the bottom of an ovenproof dish, spoon over some sauce then add a layer of ham. Repeat these layers, finishing with a layer of sauce and a scattering parmesan.

  4. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes, or until golden-brown.
