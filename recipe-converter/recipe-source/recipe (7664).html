Rhubarb, redcurrant and vanilla jam

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 568ml/1 pint jam

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

This English garden jam is particularly lovely easy to make and delicious with scones or as a filling for Victoria sponge.

By Alan Thorpe
From The Big Allotment Challenge

Ingredients

  • 250g/9oz redcurrants
  • 350g/12oz rhubarb, trimmed, cut into 2.5cm/1in slices
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds only
  • 500g/1lb 2oz granulated sugar
  • 1 small lemon, juice only

Method

  1. Put the redcurrants in a saucepan over a medium heat and add a generous splash of boiling water. Heat until soft.

  2. Meanwhile, heat the rhubarb, vanilla seeds and sugar in a separate saucepan over a low heat until the sugar has dissolved and the rhubarb has softened.

  3. Strain the redcurrants through a fine sieve into the rhubarb pan. Stir in the lemon juice.

  4. Bring the mixture to the boil, then continue to heat until its temperature reaches 105C (use a sugar thermometer for this; it will take about 20 minutes). Scoop away any scum that rises to the surface using a metal spoon. (CAUTION: hot sugar can be dangerous. Keep your eye on the pot at all times.)

  5. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside to cool for 20 minutes.

  6. Pour the jam into sterilised jars and seal. Set aside until completely cooled.

  7. This jam will keep for up to 6 months.
