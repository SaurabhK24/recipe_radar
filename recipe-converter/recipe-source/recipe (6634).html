Peanut chicken goujons with chilli pak choi and lime and coriander dressing

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 1

Recommended by

1 person

By Maria Elia
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the peanut chicken

  • 55g/2oz peanuts, shelled
  • ½ chicken breast, skin removed, sliced into strips
  • 3 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

For the pak choi

  • 2 pak choi, split in half lengthways
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ red chilli, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ lime, juice only

For the dressing

  • 2 limes, juice only
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ red chilli, chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander

Method

  1. For the peanut chicken, place the peanuts into a food processor and pulse to crumbs. Tip onto a large plate.

  2. Dust the chicken in the flour, then dip into the beaten egg and finally roll in the peanut crumbs. Heat the oil in a frying pan and fry the goujons, in batches, for 2-3 minutes on both sides, or until golden-brown, crisp and cooked through.

  3. For the pak choi, mix all of the pak choi ingredients together in a bowl. Heat a chargrill pan until smoking, then tip the pak choi mixture in and cook for 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, or until the pak choi is wilted and tender.

  4. For the dressing, combine all of the dressing ingredients together in a bowl and mix well.

  5. Serve the pak choi alongside the peanut chicken, with the dressing spooned around the edges of the plate.
