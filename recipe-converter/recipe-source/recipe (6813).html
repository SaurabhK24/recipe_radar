Pickled pears

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 2 x 1.5 litre jars

Recommended by

4 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

Pickled pears are ever so easy to make, all they need is a little time. Make them ahead of Christmas for a wonderful accompaniment to cold cuts or as a unique gift.

By The Hairy Bikers
From The Hairy Bikers' Twelve Days of Christmas

Ingredients

  • 1 litre/1¾ pints white wine vinegar
  • 0.5 litres/17½fl oz water
  • 500g/1lb 2oz caster sugar
  • 3 star anise
  • 1 large cinnamon stick, broken into three pieces
  • 1 tbsp allspice berries
  • 1 lemon, zest peeled off in large strips using a potato peeler
  • ½ tsp cloves
  • 2 tsp juniper berries
  • 20 Conference pears, peeled, stalks intact
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary

Method

  1. Bring the water, vinegar, sugar, star anise, cinnamon sticks, allspice berries, lemon zest, cloves and juniper berries to the boil in a large, lidded, non-reactive pan.

  2. Add the pears, then cover the pan with a lid and reduce the heat until the mixture is gently simmering. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the pears are tender. Remove the pears from the poaching liquid using a slotted spoon and set aside.

  3. Return the poaching liquid to the boil and boil for 4-5 minutes, or until the volume of liquid has reduced and thickened.

  4. Divide the poached pears between two 1.5 litre/2 pints 13fl oz sterilised airtight jars. Pour half of the reduced cooking liquid into each jar, making sure the spices are evenly distributed between each jar. Add one sprig of rosemary to each jar. Seal the jars tightly. The pickled pears can be stored for up two months, but can be eaten after one week. Once opened, store in the fridge and eat within two weeks.

Recipe Tips

The pickled pears can be stored for up two months.
