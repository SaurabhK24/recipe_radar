Vodka and cranberry jelly

Preparation time

overnight

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 8

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • vegetable oil, for greasing
  • 250ml/9fl oz water
  • 250g/9oz caster sugar
  • 250ml/9fl oz cranberry juice
  • 25ml/1fl oz vodka
  • 3 gelatine leaves, soaked in 3 tbsp cold water
  • 100g/3½oz blueberries
  • 8 fresh basil leaves

For the sauce

  • 200ml/7fl oz Marsala wine
  • 125g/4oz icing sugar
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds scraped out
  • 250g/9fl oz blackberries
  • double cream, to serve

Method

  1. Grease eight small jelly moulds of your choice and line with cling film.

  2. Place the water, sugar and cranberry juice into a pan and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for five minutes, then remove from the heat.

  3. Add the vodka and stir to combine.

  4. Remove the gelatine leaves from their soaking water and place into the pan with the cranberry mixture.

  5. Stir the cranberry mixture until it begins to thicken, then pour the mixture into a bowl.

  6. Place a few blueberries into the bottom of each lined jelly mould.

  7. Top up the moulds with the jelly mix, then place a basil leaf on top of each.

  8. Transfer the jellies to the fridge to chill overnight and set.

  9. For the sauce, mix the Marsala, sugar and vanilla seeds in a clean bowl. Add the blackberries and leave for two hours.

  10. To serve, turn out each jelly onto a small serving plate. Spoon equal amounts of the marinated fruit around each jelly and drizzle with a little double cream.
