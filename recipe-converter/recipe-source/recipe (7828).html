Roast river trout with toasted almonds, French beans and buerre noisette

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Earthy brown trout served with a nutty almond butter makes a deliciously quick and special meal.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 1 large brown river trout, gutted, cleaned and scaled
  • drizzle of olive oil
  • 400g/14oz French beans, trimmed
  • 4 tbsp flaked almonds
  • 110g/4oz unsalted butter
  • juice of ½ lemon
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the salad

  • 50g/1¾oz bull’s blood leaves
  • 50g/1¾oz mizuna leaves, central leaves retained
  • 50g/1¾oz baby spinach leaves
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp raspberry vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 4. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Lay the fish on the tray, season with salt and pepper drizzle with oil. Bake for 20-30 minutes, depending on the size of the fish, or until the flesh flakes easily.

  2. Meanwhile, put the beans in a steamer and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until cooked.

  3. Toast the almonds in a hot frying pan for 2-3 minutes. Transfer to a plate. Return the pan to the heat, add the butter and cook until it turns nut-brown. Stir in the lemon juice, toasted almonds and a pinch of salt and pepper, then remove from the heat.

  4. For the salad, put the leaves in a large salad bowl. Put the oil, vinegar and mustard in a small bowl and whisk.

  5. Put the fish on a large serving plate and peel back the skin. Pour the buerre noisette over the fish. Pour the dressing over the salad and mix. Serve the fish with the beans and salad.
