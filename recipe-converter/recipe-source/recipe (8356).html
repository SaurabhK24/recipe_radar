Salt baked potatoes with chorizo mayonnaise and escabeche of vegetables

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

As any crisp lover will assure you, salt and potatoes are a great combination - and baked spuds are no exception. Glynn does his in a salt crust for extra crisp skins and fluffy flesh.

By Glynn Purnell
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the salt baked potatoes

  • 650g/1lb 7oz rock salt
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 egg whites
  • 4 medium waxy potatoes, washed, skin on

For the chorizo mayonnaise

  • 400g/14oz cooked chorizo, diced
  • 300ml/10fl oz vegetable or rapeseed oil
  • 3 free-range egg yolks
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar

For the escabeche of vegetables

  • 1 medium beetroot
  • 1 medium turnip
  • 1 medium candy striped beetroot
  • 1 small fennel (not baby)
  • 1 golden beetroot
  • 100ml/3½fl oz Chardonnay vinegar
  • 300ml/10½fl oz vegetable or rapeseed oil
  • 3 sprigs thyme, leaves picked
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 1 bunch watercress
  • table salt and cracked black pepper, to season

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. For the salt baked potatoes, mix the salt, paprika and egg whites together. Cover the bottom of an ovenproof dish with the mixture and place the potatoes in the dish. Completely cover with the remaining salt mixture and bake for 40 minutes.

  3. For the chorizo mayonnaise, gently fry the chorizo over a low heat in a deep pan (don’t colour too much). Add the oil and cook slowly for 5-10 minutes.

  4. Leave to cool for 10 minutes, then pass through a fine sieve to separate the meat and oil, retain both.

  5. Whisk together the egg yolks and white wine vinegar. Slowly add the reserved oil in a thin stream, whisking all the time, until the mixture has emulsified and resembles mayonnaise. Set aside.

  6. For the escabeche of vegetables, thinly slice all the vegetables on a mandolin and place in a large bowl of ice-cold water.

  7. Mix the Chardonnay vinegar and oil to make vinaigrette. Add the thyme and garlic. Drain the vegetables and add to the vinaigrette.

  8. To serve, spoon out the mayonnaise and swirl into a circle in the centre of each plate. Dress the plate with the vegetables and garnish with watercress. Sprinkle with the reserved chorizo and season with black pepper.

  9. Turn out the salt baked potatoes and smash the salt to release the potatoes. Serve alongside the mayonnaise and vegetables.
