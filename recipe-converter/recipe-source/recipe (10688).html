Vanilla and cider poached figs with thyme yoghurt

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 1

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the figs

  • 2 fresh figs
  • 1 vanilla pod, seeds scraped out
  • 2 tbsp cider
  • vegetable oil, for greasing

For the thyme yoghurt

  • 2 tbsp Greek-style yoghurt
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves only
  • squeeze lime juice
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Method

  1. For the figs, cut the stalk off the very top of the figs and use a sharp knife to make a cross in the top of each fig. Squeeze gently to open them up slightly.

  2. Place the figs onto a square of foil and scrunch the sides up to make a parcel.

  3. Add the vanilla pod to the parcel and place the vanilla seeds inside the figs. Drizzle over the cider, then seal the top of the parcel.

  4. Rub the base of the parcel with a little vegetable oil, then place into a hot frying pan. Cook for 4-5 minutes, or until the figs have softened.

  5. For the thyme yoghurt, mix the yoghurt, thyme leaves, lime juice and vanilla extract together in a bowl.

  6. To serve, place the foil parcel into a serving bowl and open the parcel at the table. Place a dollop of the thyme yoghurt in the middle of the parcel.
