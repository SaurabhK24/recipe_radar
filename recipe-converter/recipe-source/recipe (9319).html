Spicy crab cakes

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 6

Crab cakes are definitely worth adding to your recipe repertoire and these are perfect for a dinner party. Serve with tamarind sauce or a mint raita.

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

For the spice mix

  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 6 black peppercorns
  • 1 clove

For the crab cakes

  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut
  • 225g/8oz mashed potato
  • 225g/8oz crabmeat
  • 2 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh coriander
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint
  • 1 tbsp peas
  • 100g/3½oz plain flour
  • 2 free-range eggs, beaten
  • 100g/3½oz breadcrumbs
  • olive oil, for frying

Method

  1. Heat a frying pan over a medium heat. Add the spices and fry for 2-3 minutes, or until aromatic. Transfer the fried spices to a pestle and mortar and grind to a powder.

  2. Mix together the spices, coconut, crabmeat, mashed potato, spring onions, herbs and peas in a large mixing bowl until well combined. Divide into six and shape into patties.

  3. Place the flour, beaten eggs and breadcrumbs in separate shallow bowls. Dredge the crab cakes in flour, then dip into the egg and finally coat in breadcrumbs

  4. Heat 1cm/½in of olive oil in a frying pan and fry the crab cakes for three minutes on each side. Remove and drain on kitchen paper.
