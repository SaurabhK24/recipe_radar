Scallop and lobster curry with tamarind sauce and steamed rice

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Prepare your own spice paste and you are on course for a deliciously luxurious seafood curry that’s ready in no time.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the curry

  • 3 small onions, finely chopped
  • 20g/¾oz grated fresh root ginger
  • 12 curry leaves
  • 3 tbsp grated fresh coconut
  • 5 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 2 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 8 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 1 litre/1¾ pints vegetable stock
  • 3 dates, chopped
  • 200ml/7fl oz coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 75g/2½oz unsalted butter
  • 8 scallops, removed from their shells and cleaned, roe removed and discarded
  • 1 lobster, cooked and meat removed

For the rice

  • 200g/7oz basmati rice
  • salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander

To serve

  • 2 tbsp red amaranth cress
  • 2 tbsp coriander cress

Method

  1. For the curry, first make the spice paste. In a blender, combine the seeds, onion, ginger, curry leaves, coconut, cumin seeds, peppercorns and a little water. Blend, adding more water if needed, until a smooth paste is formed.

  2. To make the tamarind stock, place the pulp, stock and spices in a large pan and simmer for 20-30 minutes.

  3. Heat a wok and add the spice paste. Once hot, add 200ml/7fl oz tamarind stock, the dates and coconut milk and cook for 4-6 minutes.

  4. Heat the butter In a frying pan. Add the scallops and lobster, and cook the scallops on each side for 1-2 minutes. Stir the cooked seafood into the curry sauce and cook for a further 1-2 minutes.

  5. For the rice, put the rice in a saucepan and season with salt. Pour cold water over the top until it comes 2cm/1in over the rice and cook for about 15 minutes, checking it doesn’t boil dry, until the water has been absorbed. Cover with a lid and turn the heat down low. After 5 minutes turn the heat off and allow the rice to steam until cooked to your liking.

  6. Serve the curry in shallow bowls garnished with the cress. Place the steamed rice in separate bowls and top with the chopped coriander.
