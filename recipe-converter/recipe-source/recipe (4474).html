Honey-battered whiting with chickpea dip

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 4

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

For the whiting

  • 60g/2oz plain flour
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried yeast
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 60ml/2fl oz cold water
  • 60ml/2fl oz hot water
  • vegetable oil, for deep-frying
  • 500g/1lb 2oz whiting, cut into goujons

For the hummus

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 1 large handful fresh coriander
  • 125ml/4½fl oz olive oil
  • 3 limes, juice only
  • 1 red chilli, chopped
  • ½ red onion, peeled and finely chopped

Method

  1. For the whiting, mix together the flour, smoked paprika and yeast in a bowl.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the egg, one tablespoon of the honey and the cold water until well combined. Add this to the flour mixture and whisk well to form a batter.

  3. In a separate small bowl, combine the remaining honey with the hot water to make a dressing. Set aside.

  4. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep heavy-based frying pan until a breadcrumb sizzles and turns golden-brown when dropped into it. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended.)

  5. Pat the fish dry with kitchen paper, then coat in the batter and deep fry, in batches, in the hot oil for 5-6 minutes, or until golden-brown and crisp.

  6. For the hummus, place all of the hummus ingredients into a food processor and blend to a smooth purée. Spoon into a small serving bowl for dipping.

  7. Place the whiting goujons on a serving dish and drizzle over the honey dressing. Serve with the bowl of hummus on the side.
