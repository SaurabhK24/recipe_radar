Seared venison salad

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

1 person

A light and healthy way to serve venison - a plentiful, sustainable, free range meat that's packed with flavour.

By Valentine Warner
From Great British Food Revival

Ingredients

  • olive oil, for frying
  • 500g/17oz venison loin (preferably from a roe deer)

For the salad dressing

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 5 salted anchovies, in oil
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp pickling juice from a jar of picked walnuts (see below)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp walnut oil
  • freshly ground black pepper

For the salad

  • 1 bunch hot radishes (preferably French), sliced
  • handful fresh chervil, leaves only, chopped
  • handful fresh pea shoots
  • dash walnut oil
  • 1 lemon, juice only
  • pinch salt

To serve

  • 3-4 baby beetroots, peeled, sliced
  • 4 gherkins, drained, sliced
  • 4 pickled walnuts, chopped

Method

  1. For the venison, heat a little oil in a frying pan over a medium-to-high heat. Add the venison loin and fry, turning regularly, for 10-12 minutes, until browned all over but still pink inside. Set the meat aside to rest.

  2. For the salad dressing, heat a separate frying pan over a medium heat. Melt the butter, then drain the oil form the anchovies and add the anchovies to the melted butter. Continue to cook until the butter has melted and the anchovies have collapsed.

  3. Add the Worcestershire sauce, walnut-pickling juice and brown sugar. Stir well and continue to cook until the mixture reduces to a syrupy consistency. Stir in the walnut oil until well combined, then season, to taste, with freshly ground black pepper. Keep warm until ready to serve.

  4. Meanwhile, for the salad, in a large bowl, mix together the radishes, chervil and pea shoots, then drizzle over the walnut oil and lemon juice, stirring well to combine. Season to taste with salt.

  5. Once the venison has rested, carve into slices.

  6. To serve, arrange a few of the beetroot and gherkin slices onto each of four serving plates. Sprinkle over the chopped pickled walnuts. Arrange the venison slices on top. Heap the pea shoot and radish salad on top of the meat. Drizzle over the warm salad dressing.

Recipe Tips

To prevent the beetroot from staining your hands as you peel them, wear disposable latex
gloves.
