Herbed venison cutlets with skordalia and beetroot and jalapeno relish

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

2 people

Skordalia is a garlicky Greek mashed potato dish often served as a dip or with grilled meats. John Torode's version is less pungent than some, but delicious all the same.

By John Torode
From MasterChef

Ingredients

For the relish

  • 4 tsp olive oil
  • 4 small beetroot, cooked, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 small shallot, roughly chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, roughly chopped
  • 2 stalks flatleaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • 2 stalks coriander, roughly chopped
  • 50g/1¾oz shelled walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • 2 tsp caster sugar

For the skordalia

  • 250g/9oz potatoes, peeled
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 100ml/3½fl oz milk
  • 100ml/3½fl oz olive oil
  • salt and white pepper
  • 1 lemon, juice only
  • handful flatleaf parsley, roughly chopped

For the venison cutlets

  • 100g/3½oz plain flour
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 200g/7oz fine dried breadcrumbs
  • small handful chopped tarragon, flatleaf parsley, sage and chervil
  • 2 free-range eggs, beaten with a little milk
  • 12 venison cutlets (from the centre of the rack)
  • vegetable oil, for frying

Method

  1. For the relish, heat the oil in a frying pan and cook the chopped ingredients over a low heat for 15 minutes. Add the sherry vinegar and sugar and cook for a few minutes until glossy. Add 50ml/2fl oz water and continue to cook until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Transfer to a food processor and blend to a smooth purée. Set aside until ready to serve.

  3. For the skordalia, place the potatoes in a heavy-based pan with the garlic, milk and olive oil, and season to taste with salt and pepper. Place over a medium heat, bring to the boil and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are soft.

  4. Remove from the heat and drain the potatoes, reserving the cooking liquor. Place the potatoes in a food processor and blend with the lemon juice and half of the cooking liquid. Return to the saucepan and fold in the parsley.

  5. For the venison cutlets, put the flour in a bowl and season generously with salt and pepper. Spread the breadcrumbs on a plate and mix in the chopped herbs.

  6. Place the beaten egg in a shallow bowl. Place the flour, beaten egg and breadcrumbs in a line ready for coating the cutlets. Roll the venison cutlets in the flour (including the bone), dip in the egg and coat with the breadcrumbs. Make sure the cutlets are evenly coated all over and the crumbs patted down (to help them stick to the cutlets).

  7. Preheat the oven to 150C/300F/Gas 2.

  8. Set a large heavy-based pan over a medium heat. Add two tablespoons of oil and, once the oil is hot, add the cutlets (you should fit six in a pan, but cook in smaller batches if necessary). Fry for 2-3 minutes each side, or until golden-brown and cooked to your liking. Once cooked place in the oven to keep warm while you cook the remaining cutlets.

  9. To serve, reheat the skordalia and, when warm, divide among serving plates. Place three cutlets on top and serve with a quenelle of relish.
