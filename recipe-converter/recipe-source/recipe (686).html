Beef, venison and oyster pie

Preparation time

overnight

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 6

Recommended by

2 people

Tempt your taste buds with a classic British pie recipe. Serve with a dollop of mash and green vegetables.

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 150ml/5fl oz Irish stout
  • 100ml/3½fl oz red wine
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 450g/1lb braising steak, choped
  • 300g/10½oz venison shoulder, chopped
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 50g/2oz unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 litre/1¾ pints beef stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 8 oysters, shucked
  • 400g/14oz ready-made shortcrust pastry
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten

Method

  1. Mix the stout, wine, garlic and thyme in a bowl until well combined. Add the steak and venison. Cover and chill in the fridge overnight.

  2. Remove the meat from the marinade and dredge it in half of the flour.

  3. Heat half of the butter in a large heavy-based saucepan and brown the meat on all sides for 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.

  4. Add the rest of the butter and fry the onion for 3-4 minutes. Add the remaining flour and cook for two minutes. Stir in the tomato purée and cook for a further six minutes.

  5. Pour in the marinade a little at a time followed by the stock. Boil the mixture for 10-15 minutes, or until it becomes a thick sauce.

  6. Add the meat to the pan with the bay leaf and cook for at least two hours, or until the meat is very tender. Set aside to cool, then stir in the oysters.

  7. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  8. Roll out two-thirds of the pastry on a floured work surface and use it to line a large pie dish. Spoon the meat mixture into the pie dish.

  9. Roll out the remaining pastry and use it to cover the meat mixture. Trim the pastry and crimp the edges. Brush the top of the pie with the beaten egg. Cut a small hole in the top of the pie to allow steam to escape.

  10. Bake in the oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is crisp and golden-brown and filling is piping hot.
