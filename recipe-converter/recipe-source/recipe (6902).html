Plaice with shellfish and wild garlic

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

If you are a fish lover then this is the recipe for you. Plaice topped with shellfish made extra special by the addition of wild garlic and herbs.

By Tom Kitchin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 2 whole plaice
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 200ml/7fl oz white wine
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 200g/7oz mussels, cleaned and de-bearded (discard any that don't close when gently tapped)
  • 200g/7oz clams, cleaned
  • knob of butter
  • 100g/3½oz squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 50g/1¾oz winkles, cleaned
  • 50g/1¾oz brown shrimp, cleaned
  • 1 lemon, juice only
  • bunch of wild garlic

To serve

  • wild herbs, to garnish

Method

  1. Prepare the plaice, trim off the fins, head and tail, take off the dark skin and leaving the white skin on the underside.

  2. Heat a large lidded saucepan and add the olive oil, once hot add the shallots, wine and parsley along with the mussels, clams and parsley and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the mussels and clams they have opened.

  3. Remove the mussels and clams and set aside. Discard any that have not opened. Simmer the remaining liquid and cook until the volume of liquid has reduced by two thirds.

  4. Heat a non-stick frying pan, add the remaining olive oil and cook the plaice for 6-8 minutes.

  5. Gently warm the shellfish reduction over a low heat and whisk in the butter. Once the sauce has emulsified, blend using a stick blender and then reduce further. Add the squid, winkles, brown shrimp, lemon juice and wild garlic. Simmer for 1-2 minutes until the squid is cooked.

  6. To serve, put the plaice, shellfish and sauce on serving plate and garnish with the wild herbs
