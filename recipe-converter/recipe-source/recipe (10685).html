Vanilla panna cotta, roasted watermelon, chilled ginger, mint and melon soup

Preparation time

over 2 hours

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 6

Lightly caramelised watermelon is a revelation as an accompaniment to vanilla-rich panna cotta and melon soup in this showy dinner party dessert recipe.

By Richard Phillips
From Chefs: Put Your Menu Where Your Mouth Is

Ingredients

For the vanilla panna cotta

  • 1 litre/1¾pints double cream
  • 135g/4¾oz caster sugar
  • 7 vanilla pods
  • 3 gelatine leaves (soaked in a bowl with cold water to soften)

For the ‘roasted’ watermelon

  • 1 watermelon, cut into 3cm/1¼in cubes (keep any excess, misshapen, watermelon for the soup)

For the soup

  • reserved watermelon
  • 100g/3½oz caster sugar
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger

Method

  1. For the vanilla panna cotta, put the cream and sugar into a saucepan.

  2. Split the vanilla pods, using a sharp knife scrape the seeds into the cream, and add the pods. Bring to the boil.

  3. Squeeze any excess water from the soaked gelatin. Add it to the hot cream and whisk to dissolve. Pour the mixture into six individual dariole moulds.

  4. Place into the fridge and leave to set.

  5. For the ‘roasted’ watermelon, fry the watermelon in a pan until coloured all over.

  6. For the soup, blend all the excess watermelon in a blender or food processor.

  7. In a pan, heat the sugar and 100ml/3½fl oz water until the sugar dissolves. Leave to cool down. Stir in the mint, ground ginger and blended watermelon and put in the fridge to chill.

  8. Once the panna cotta has set, turn each one out onto a plate.

  9. Serve with the chilled soup and caramelised watermelon chunks.
