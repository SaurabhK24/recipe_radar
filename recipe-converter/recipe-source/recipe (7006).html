Pomegranate panna cotta with pomegranate and orange caramel

Preparation time

over 2 hours

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Try this fruity dessert in the winter months, when pomegranate is in season.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the panna cotta

  • 2 leaves gelatine
  • 110ml/4fl oz pomegranate juice
  • 75g/3oz caster sugar
  • 1 tsp orange zest
  • 500ml/18fl oz double cream
  • 1 pomegranate, seeds only

For the pomegranate caramel

  • 2 oranges, peeled and segmented
  • 1 pomegranate, seeds only
  • 4 tbsp caster sugar
  • 4 tbsp pomegranate juice

Method

  1. For the panna cotta, soak the gelatine leaves in a bowl of cold water until softened and pliable, about 3-4 minutes.

  2. Meanwhile, place the pomegranate juice into a small pan and gently heat, before removing from the heat.

  3. Remove the gelatine from the water, squeezing out any excess liquid, and add the leaves to the hot pomegranate juice. Stir until dissolved.

  4. Meanwhile, place the sugar, orange zest and 350ml/12fl oz of the cream into a saucepan and bring to a simmer, stirring to dissolve all the sugar.

  5. Pour the pomegranate juice into the cream mixture, stirring all the time. Set aside to cool a little.

  6. Place the remaining cream into a bowl and whisk until soft peaks form when the whisk is removed from the bowl.

  7. Fold the whisked cream into the set-aside mixture. Pour the mixture through a sieve into a jug.

  8. Arrange the pomegranate seeds on the base of four dariole moulds or ramekins. Place the moulds on a plate or baking sheet before pouring the mixture into each mould. Leave in the fridge for 1-2 hours, or until set.

  9. For the pomegranate caramel, heat a frying pan until hot, add the orange segments and sear briefly. Remove the orange from the pan and set aside.

  10. Add the pomegranate seeds, sugar and juice to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes until thickened and the sugar has melted. Return the oranges to the pan and keep warm.

  11. To serve, remove the moulds from the fridge and dip the base of each mould into a bowl of boiled water briefly to help loosen it.

  12. Gently slide a knife around the edge of each panna cotta and turn out onto a plate. Spoon the pomegranate caramel around the side and serve at once.
