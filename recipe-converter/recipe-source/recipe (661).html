Beef fillet medallions, wild mushroom brandy cream, green bean fricassée and champ

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Who can resist beef fillet served with a creamy mushroom and brandy sauce, crunchy green beans and a mound of Irish champ to mop up the juices?

By James Tanner
From Chefs: Put Your Menu Where Your Mouth Is

Ingredients

For the beef and sauce

  • vegetable oil, for frying
  • 3 shallots, finely chopped
  • 5 tbsp brandy
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 180g/6oz mixed chestnut and wild mushrooms, sliced
  • 500ml/18fl oz beef stock
  • 3 sprigs thyme, leaves only
  • splash double cream
  • 600g/1lb 5 oz beef fillet, cut into medallions
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the champ

  • 400g/14oz potatoes, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 150ml/5fl oz milk
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • 30g/1oz butter

For the fricassée

  • 300g/10½oz green beans
  • 2 shallot, finely sliced
  • dash olive oil

Method

  1. For the beef and sauce, heat a little vegetable oil in a pan and add the shallots and garlic. Cook until lightly coloured.

  2. Add the mushrooms and fry until cooked through.

  3. Add the brandy and cook until the volume of liquid has reduced by half.

  4. Add the stock and the thyme and, again, cook until the volume of liquid has reduced by half.

  5. Add the cream and cook until the mixture has reduced to a sauce consistency.

  6. Meanwhile, for the champ, place the potatoes in a pan of water and bring to the boil. Cook for 15-20 minutes, or until tender.

  7. For the beef and sauce, put a large frying pan on a medium heat. Season the beef medallions, to taste, with salt and pepper. Rub with oil and fry on each side for at least two minutes, depending on how well done you enjoy your fillet. Keep warm until ready to serve.

  8. For the champ, when the potatoes are cooked, drain well and mash until smooth.

  9. Put the milk and spring onion into a small pan and bring to the boil. Simmer until the onions are cooked, then pass the mixture through a fine sieve. Stir the milk into the mashed potatoes and keep warm until ready to serve.

  10. For the fricassée, bring a pan of water to the boil, add the green beans and simmer for four minutes. Prepare a bowl of ice cold water.

  11. Remove the beans from the heat, drain and briefly place into the cold water. Drain again.

  12. In a pan, lightly fry the shallot in a little oil and add the green beans. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  13. Serve the beef with the sauce, bean fricassee and champ.
