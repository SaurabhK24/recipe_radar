Corned beef pot pies

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

18 people

Simon Rimmer uses this retro ingredient to make comforting pot pies. Serve with a mound of mashed potato.

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 200g/7oz smoked bacon lardons
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 200ml/7fl oz bitter or ale
  • 200ml/7fl oz beef stock
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 500g/1lb 2oz tinned corned beef, cut into cubes
  • 6 gherkins, chopped
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • salt and white pepper
  • 1 x 375g/13oz packet readymade puff pastry
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. Heat the oil in a frying pan and fry the bacon lardons for 4-5 minutes, or until crisp. Remove from the pan with a slotted spoon and set aside.

  3. In the same pan, fry the onions and garlic on a medium heat for 5-6 minutes, or until softened. Add the cooked bacon, carrots, bitter, beef stock, sugar, corned beef and gherkins. Bring the mixture to the boil, reduce the heat and simmer for 25 minutes.

  4. Remove the pan from the heat, stir in the parsley and season to taste with salt and white pepper. Divide the mixture among four individual pie dishes and set aside to cool completely.

  5. Cut four circles out of the puff pastry that are large enough to cover the pie dishes. Place the pastry on top of the pie dishes and brush with the beaten egg.

  6. Place the pies onto a baking tray and bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is crisp and golden-brown.

Recipe Tips

You can also make this recipe in one large pie dish.
