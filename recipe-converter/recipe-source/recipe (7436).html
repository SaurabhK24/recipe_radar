Quick chicken liver pâté

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4-5

Recommended by

5 people

This easy pâté makes a stunning starter for a dinner party, or another special event like Christmas dinner.

By Antony Worrall Thompson
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 175g/6oz unsalted butter, softened
  • 450g/1lb chicken livers, trimmed and cleaned
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp soft thyme leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp brandy
  • 2 tsp anchovy essence
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 55g/2oz clarified butter, melted

Method

  1. Heat 15g/½oz of the butter in a frying pan until foaming.

  2. Add half the livers and fry for 7-8 minutes, or until golden-brown and cooked through. Repeat with a second batch of butter and livers.

  3. Place the livers and juices in a food processor.

  4. In the same pan heat another 15g/½oz butter, add the shallot, thyme and garlic, and cook over a moderate heat until the shallot is soft but not coloured.

  5. Add the brandy, anchovy essence and pepper, and scrape the bottom of the pan to release any coagulated juices.

  6. Place everything in the food processor, including the remaining unsalted butter. Blend until smooth.

  7. If you want a very smooth pâté, pass the mixture through a fine sieve and put in a bowl. Cover with the cling film, cool then refrigerate. If not using within 48 hours, cover the top with clarified butter.
