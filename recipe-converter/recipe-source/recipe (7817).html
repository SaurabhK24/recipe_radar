Roast pheasant with celeriac and bacon

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

12 people

Bacon and celeriac are perfect foil to the full flavor of roast pheasant in this delicious game recipe.

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

For the pheasants

  • 2 x pheasants
  • 100g/3½oz unsalted butter
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 rashers smoked streaky bacon
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 250g/9oz chestnut mushrooms, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 leek, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • small handful fresh thyme leaves, chopped

For the sauce

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 150g/5oz bacon lardons
  • 1 leek, sliced
  • 1 celeriac, cubed
  • 200ml/7fl oz cider
  • 100ml/3½fl oz game stock
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves, chopped
  • 100ml/3½fl oz double cream

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. Place 25g/1oz of butter inside each pheasant. Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Place a rasher of bacon and a bay leaf on each bird.

  3. Put the onion, mushrooms, carrot, leek, garlic cloves and thyme in a roasting tin and place the birds on top.

  4. Spread more butter over the birds and roast in the oven for about 45 minutes.

  5. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a large heavy-based frying pan over a medium heat. Add the bacon lardons and fry until crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon.

  6. Add the leek and celeriac and cook for 6-8 minutes. Return the bacon to the pan. Addthe cider and continue to cook until the volume of the liquid has reduced by half. Add the stock and reduce by half again.

  7. Stir in the cream and thyme leaves. Season to taste with freshly ground black pepper.

  8. To serve, carve the pheasants and place onto serving plates. Spoon the roasted vegetables alongside and spoon over the bacon and celeriac.
