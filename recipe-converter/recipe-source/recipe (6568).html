Paul’s mum’s jam tarts

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 20

Recommended by

15 people

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

This ‘incredibly difficult’ recipe was sent in by my mum. You can use any jam in the tarts, whichever suits your taste buds best. Top tip from my mum: it’s a good way to use up leftover pastry. I hope I do you proud mum!

By Paul Hollywood
From Paul Hollywood's Pies & Puds

Ingredients

  • 85g/3oz unsalted butter or margarine
  • 170g/6oz self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 20 tsp jam, or to taste

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. Rub together the butter or margarine with the flour in a bowl until it resembles breadcrumbs. Stir in a few tablespoons of water to form a dough.

  3. Roll out on a lightly floured surface. Use a pastry cutter to cut out about 15 circular shapes.

  4. Put each pastry case into an individual cupcake tray. Add a teaspoon of jam to each case – not too much or it will ooze everywhere.

  5. If there is any leftover pastry, re-roll it and cut smaller shapes, either circles or stars, and put over the jam to act as the lids.

  6. Put the tray of tarts into the oven and cook for about 30 minutes, or until the pastry has gone pale brown (check the tarts after 20 minutes).
