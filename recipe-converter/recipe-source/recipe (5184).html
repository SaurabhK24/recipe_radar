Lemon, ricotta and potato cake

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 8

Recommended by

1 person

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 225g/8oz butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 350g/12¼oz caster sugar
  • 250g/9oz mashed potato
  • 250g/9oz ricotta
  • 4 free-range eggs
  • 125g/4½oz flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 lemons, juice and grated zest
  • pinch ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds
  • crème fraîche, to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 5. Grease a 24-26cm/9-10in loose-bottomed cake tin with butter.

  2. Cream the butter and sugar together in a bowl until pale and fluffy.

  3. Whisk in the mashed potato and ricotta using an electric whisk for 4-5 minutes, or until very fluffy.

  4. Crack in the eggs, one at a time, and whisk again until fully incorporated into the mixture.

  5. Sift the flour and baking powder together, then fold the mixture into the cake batter until well combined.

  6. Fold in the lemon juice, lemon zest, cinnamon and poppy seeds until well combined.

  7. Spoon the cake batter into the prepared cake tin. Bake for 1-1¼ hours, checking regularly, until a skewer inserted into the centre of the cake comes out clean. (If the top of the cake starts to darken too much during cooking, cover it loosely with aluminium foil.)

  8. Set the cake aside to cool slightly, then serve with crème fraîche.
