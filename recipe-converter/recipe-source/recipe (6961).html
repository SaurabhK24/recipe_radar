Poached fillet of beef with stout gravy

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

1 person

By Mike Robinson
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 600g/1lb 5oz beef fillet
  • 12 shallots, peeled
  • 2 red onions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 3 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 200ml/7fl oz Irish stout
  • 200ml/7fl oz beef stock
  • 3 sprigs of thyme
  • salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 25g/1oz butter
  • 1 tbsp flour

For the cheesy chive mash

  • mashed potato, to serve
  • 2 tbsp chives, chopped, to garnish
  • parmesan cheese

Method

  1. Heat a frying pan until hot. Add in half a tablespoon of olive oil and heat through. Add in the beef and sear on both sides.

  2. Heat the remaining olive oil in a casserole dish. Add in the shallots, red onion, tomato purée and garlic and fry, stirring often, until lightly browned.

  3. Add in the beef fillet, stout, stock and thyme. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  4. Bring to the boil, reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes.

  5. Remove the beef fillet and set aside to rest, keeping warm.

  6. Bring the stout mixture to the boil. Rub the butter and flour together until combined and add to the sauce, a little at a time, stirring until absorbed, to thicken the sauce.

  7. Make the mash by combining the chives and parmesan to taste.

  8. Serve the beef with the cheesy, chive mash and spoon around the Guinness and shallot gravy.
