Apple and Sauternes bread and butter pudding

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

Dietary

 

Vegetarian

By Paul Rankin
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

  • 4 slices brioche, chopped
  • 300ml/½ pint fresh ready-made vanilla custard
  • 1 free-range egg yolk
  • 75ml/2½fl oz double cream
  • 1 tbsp Sauternes wine
  • 30g/1oz butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 1 apple, peeled and cored
  • 1 tbsp icing sugar

Method

  1. Butter two small gratin dishes and place the chopped brioche into the base of each dish.

  2. Pour the custard, egg yolk, cream and Sauternes into a saucepan and warm through, whisking well. Pour half the custard mixture over the brioche.

  3. Melt the butter in a frying pan. Chop half of the apple and add to the pan, frying over a medium heat until caramelised. Divide the apple between the gratin dishes and then pour over the rest of the custard mixture. Cover each dish with cling film and microwave on high for four minutes. Set aside and leave to rest for two minutes.

  4. Thinly slice the other half of the apple into rings, place on a baking sheet and sprinkle with icing sugar. Using a blowtorch, carefully caramelise the top of each slice of apple. (Alternatively, you can place the apple slices under the grill to caramelise.)

  5. To serve, top each gratin dish with the caramelised apple rings and serve immediately.
