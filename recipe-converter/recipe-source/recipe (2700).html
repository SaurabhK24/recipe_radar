Crab and cucumber vichyssoise

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 6

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 30g/1oz butter, unsalted
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 400g/14oz potatoes, cut into cubes
  • 400ml/14fl oz vegetable stock
  • 75ml/2½fl oz white wine
  • 2 cucumbers, peeled, seeds removed, finely chopped
  • ½ lemon, juice only
  • 50ml/2fl oz double cream
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh chives
  • 250g/9oz cooked white crabmeat, picked
  • 1 radish, finely sliced, to garnish

Method

  1. Melt the butter in a pan until foaming, then add the leeks and fry for 4-5 minutes, or until tender.

  2. Add the chopped potatoes, stock and wine to the pan and bring to boil. Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  3. Transfer the mixture to a food processor and blend until smooth. (Adjust the consistency of the soup, if needed, by adding water). Chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes, or until completely cold.

  4. Stir the cucumber into the soup, then add the lemon juice, to taste. Spoon the soup into serving bowls or glasses and swirl in the double cream. Sprinkle over the chopped chives and top each serving with a spoonful of crabmeat and a few slices of radish.
