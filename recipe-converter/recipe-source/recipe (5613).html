Baked Alaska with passion fruit

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Recommended by

2 people

James Martin uses passion fruit in place of berries to produce a sophisticated version of the 70s family favourite.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the baked alaska

  • 3 medium egg whites
  • 90g/3¾oz caster sugar
  • 90g/3¾oz icing sugar, sieved
  • 1 large sponge flan case, cut in half widthways to about 5mm/¼in thick
  • 4 tbsp passion fruit liqueur
  • 500ml/18 fl oz vanilla ice cream

For the passion fruit sauce

  • 50ml/2floz water
  • 50g/1¾oz caster sugar
  • 200ml/7fl oz passion fruit purée
  • ½ tsp arrowroot
  • 2 passion fruit, halved

Method

  1. Whisk the egg whites with the caster sugar in a clean bowl until the mixture forms stiff peaks.

  2. Add the icing sugar and whisk again until the mixture forms stiff peaks and is smooth and shiny.

  3. Using a 7.5cm/3in cookie cutter, cut out four sponge discs.

  4. Place the sponges onto serving plates and spoon over the passion fruit liqueur.

  5. Top each one with a large scoop of ice cream.

  6. Transfer the meringue mixture into a piping bag and pipe the meringue around the ice cream, covering from top to bottom.

  7. Glaze the meringue with a blow torch, or briefly in a hot oven.

  8. For the passion fruit sauce, place the water and caster sugar into a saucepan and heat until the sugar has melted and the mixture is just simmering.

  9. Add the passion fruit purée and mix well then whisk in the arrowroot and cook until just thickened.

  10. Remove from the heat and stir in the seeds from the halved passion fruit.

  11. To serve, spoon the passion fruit sauce around the edge of each individual baked Alaska.
