Weeping lamb with boulangere potatoes

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4-6

Recommended by

11 people

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

  • 2.7kg/6lb leg of lamb
  • 50g/2oz butter, softened
  • 3 stems rosemary
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, cut into slivers
  • 50ml/1¾fl oz olive oil
  • 6 potatoes, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 4 onions, sliced thinly
  • salt and black pepper
  • 450ml/16fl oz chicken or lamb stock

To serve

  • 300g/11oz mixed salad leaves
  • 1 lemon, juiced

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 230C/450F/Gas 8.

  2. Rub the meat with softened butter and place it in a roasting tin.

  3. Using a small, sharp, pointed knife make a series of small deep slits about 4cm (1½in) apart all over the leg of lamb, inserting a sliver of garlic and a small sprig of rosemary in each slit.

  4. Pour a tablespoon or so of the olive oil over the meat and cook for about 30 minutes.

  5. While the meat is cooking, place the potatoes and onions in a bowl, toss together and season.

  6. After 30 minutes, take the meat from the oven and lift it out of the tin.

  7. Layer the potatoes and onions into the fat in the tin and pour over the stock.

  8. Return the roasting tin to the low shelf and place the lamb directly on the shelf immediately above - so that the juices will 'weep' onto the potatoes.

  9. Roast for about another hour depending on how well you like your meat cooked.

  10. Dress the salad leaves with the lemon and remaining olive oil, then season with salt and pepper.

  11. Carve the lamb and serve with the potatoes and salad.
