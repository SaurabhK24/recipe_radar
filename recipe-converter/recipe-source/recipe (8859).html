Skate with aniseed liqueur

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 4 small skate wings
  • 50g/2oz plain flour, seasoned with salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 75g/2½ butter
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 50g/2oz capers
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 75ml/2½ fl oz aniseed liqueur
  • 100ml/3½ fl oz fish stock
  • 1 tbsp pine nuts

To serve

  • sautéed potatoes
  • lemon wedges
  • fresh parsley, chopped

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. Dredge the fish in the seasoned flour.

  3. Melt half of the butter in a large ovenproof frying pan. Add the skate wings and fry for three minutes on each side.

  4. Transfer to the oven and cook for three minutes, or until just cooked through. Remove the skate wings from the pan and set aside on a warm plate.

  5. Add the shallots to the same frying pan over a medium heat and cook until softened.

  6. Add the capers and tomato purée and fry for two minutes.

  7. Add the aniseed liqueur and fish stock and bring to the boil. Cook for five minutes.

  8. Whisk in the remaining butter, then add the pine nuts.

  9. To serve, place a skate wing onto each plate and spoon over the sauce. Serve with sautéed potatoes, lemon wedges and a sprinkling of fresh parsley.
